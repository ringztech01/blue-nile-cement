01 — Company

About Blue Nile Cement

Blue Nile Cement Industries Ltd. was founded in 2008 as a grinding and bagging operation outside Juba, set up to reduce South Sudan's near-total dependence on cement imported overland from Kenya, Uganda, and northern Sudan.

The company draws on limestone reserves from the Kapoeta belt in Eastern Equatoria, trucked roughly 270km to our blending and packing facility on the Juba–Nimule corridor.

What began as a single grinding line producing under 60,000 tonnes a year has grown into the country's largest domestically-based cement supplier, serving contractors, government infrastructure programmes, and retail hardware distributors across all ten states.

We remain privately held, majority South Sudanese-owned, with a regional technical partnership that supports clinker sourcing during dry-season transport disruptions — and, since 2026, our own captive solar power supply.

04 — Power & Sustainability

Sun-powered production

ICC × Blue Nile Cement

Blue Nile Cement has partnered with ICC, our sister company and a regional solar and inverter systems integrator, to build a captive solar power system at the Juba–Nimule plant.

The installation combines a 64MW array serving the main grinding and blending lines with a second 51MW array dedicated to the bagging plant and site infrastructure — a combined 115MW of installed solar capacity commissioned in 2026.

The system reduces reliance on diesel generation during dry-season fuel shortages and lowers the plant's per-tonne carbon footprint, supporting both production continuity and the company's long-term sustainability targets.
